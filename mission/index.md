# Our Mission

The **Digital Heart Research Group** — part of the [Engineering Medicine Research Group](https://emg-nju.github.io/Homepage/) at the Medical School of Nanjing University — works at the intersection of computational modeling, artificial intelligence, and cardiovascular medicine.

## What we do

We build **digital twins of the human heart** — biophysically detailed, anatomically realistic, and patient-specific computational models — and use them to understand and treat **cardiac arrhythmias**.

Our work spans:

- **Multi-scale, multi-physics modeling** of cardiac electrophysiology and electromechanics, from ion channels to the whole organ.
- **Patient-specific digital twins** constructed from CT/MRI imaging and clinical data, for virtual clinical trials and personalized therapy planning (e.g., catheter ablation).
- **AI-accelerated simulation**: deep-learning surrogate models and causal-inference methods that make high-throughput, clinically useful modeling possible.
- **Mechanistic insight into arrhythmogenesis** and the screening of anti-arrhythmic drugs and cardiotoxicity.

Our goal is to bring to medicine the level of predictive impact that computational modeling has long had in engineering.

## Who we are looking for

We welcome trainees from **diverse educational backgrounds**. Cardiac digital-twin research is inherently interdisciplinary — we have benefited enormously from people trained in:

- Computer science, electronic & information engineering, biomedical engineering
- Physics, applied mathematics, mechanical engineering
- Physiology, pharmacology, cardiology, and other life-science disciplines
- Clinical medicine

What matters most is curiosity, rigor, and a willingness to learn across fields. If any of the following excites you, we would love to hear from you:

- Heart digital twins and complex-system modeling of disease
- Deep-learning surrogate models for physics-based simulators
- Causal inference for mechanistic understanding of disease
- Virtual / augmented reality for clinical and scientific applications
- High-performance computing for biomedical simulation

We recruit at all levels — **postdoctoral fellows, Ph.D. and master's students, research assistants, visiting students, and undergraduates**.
