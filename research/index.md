# Research Mission

The **Digital Heart Research Team** — part of the [Engineering Medicine Research Group](https://emg-nju.github.io/Homepage/) at the Medical School of Nanjing University — works at the intersection of computational modeling, artificial intelligence, and cardiovascular medicine.

## Mission

We build **digital twins of the human heart** — biophysically detailed, anatomically realistic, and patient-specific computational models — and use them to understand and treat **cardiac arrhythmias**.

Our goal is to bring to medicine the level of predictive impact that computational modeling has long had in engineering.

## Research Areas

{: .postsfont}
Our research integrates mechanistic modeling, multi-scale simulation, machine learning, imaging, and experimental data to advance the following areas:

- Constructing biophysically and biochemically detailed digital twins of the human heart
- Optimizing treatment strategies for heart rhythm disorders via personalized simulations
- Developing AI-accelerated simulation pipelines, including deep-learning surrogate models and causal-inference methods
- Building quantitative systems pharmacology platforms integrating iPSC-derived cardiomyocytes, computational modeling, and machine learning for anti-arrhythmic drug screening
- Dissecting electro-mechanical coupling and feedback in the heart and their contributions to arrhythmia
- Developing heart-torso models to study ventricular arrhythmias, electrocardiograms (ECGs), and responses to stressors
- Investigating sex differences in cardiac electrophysiology and arrhythmia
- Untangling subcellular signaling networks in arrhythmia mechanisms
- Studying molecular and structural mechanisms of sinus node dysfunction

## Approach

Our work spans:

- **Multi-scale, multi-physics modeling** of cardiac electrophysiology and electromechanics, from ion channels to the whole organ
- **Patient-specific digital twins** constructed from CT/MRI imaging and clinical data for virtual trials and personalized therapy planning
- **AI-enabled computational medicine**, making complex simulation more efficient and clinically actionable
- **Mechanistic investigation of arrhythmogenesis**, drug response, and cardiotoxicity

## Who We Welcome

We welcome trainees and collaborators from diverse backgrounds, including:

- Computer science, electronic and information engineering, biomedical engineering
- Physics, applied mathematics, and mechanical engineering
- Physiology, pharmacology, cardiology, and related life-science disciplines
- Clinical medicine
