**May 2026.** Our Perspective article, "Digital heart initiative: an ecosystem for digital discovery and precision medicine in cardiology," has been published in ***National Science Review***.    <!--more-->

<img src="/docs/post_images/nwag302fig1.jpeg" alt="Conceptual framework of the digital heart ecosystem" class="img-fluid">

{: .small_font}
Figure: Conceptual framework of the digital heart ecosystem.

{: .postsfont}
The article outlines a digital heart ecosystem for connecting cardiovascular data, computational tools, discovery workflows, and clinical translation. It proposes four core micro-ecosystems: **devices and data**, **digital tools**, **digital discovery and precision cardiology**, and **infrastructure and governance**.

{: .postsfont}
Together, these components create iterative flows between patient and device data, AI/ML/LLM-enabled modeling, mechanistic digital twins, hypothesis generation, clinical decision support, and the governance structures needed for reliable, privacy-preserving, and clinically accountable use. The Perspective argues that coordinated development of this ecosystem can help move cardiac digital twins from static, snapshot-based models toward longitudinal and agentic systems for precision cardiology.

[Link to the article](https://doi.org/10.1093/nsr/nwag302)
